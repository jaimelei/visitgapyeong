# Project Introduction

## Overview

This project is a tourism website for Gapyeong, South Korea.

The website is not intended to sell, advertise, or promote businesses. It is not a commerce platform, booking website, directory, or travel agency.

Its purpose is purely experiential and informational.

The goal is to introduce Gapyeong as a place that quietly exists—a refuge from city noise and modern pace. The website should communicate atmosphere, emotion, and identity before information.

The experience should feel cinematic, premium, mysterious, poetic, and memorable.

---

# Core Idea

Gapyeong should not be presented as a destination.

It should feel like:

* a place that simply exists
* silence in the midst of city noise
* somewhere time moves slower
* a place people discover rather than search for
* something remembered rather than advertised

The experience should evoke longing, nostalgia, curiosity, tranquility, and wonder.

---

# Design Direction

The website should avoid looking like:

* a travel agency
* a tourism portal
* a SaaS website
* a corporate landing page
* an ecommerce platform
* a modern startup website

The experience should instead resemble:

* an editorial magazine
* a coffee table photography book
* a scrapbook
* a museum exhibit
* an art journal
* a film

The emphasis should be on storytelling and atmosphere.

---

# Layout Constraints

Avoid conventional structures.

Avoid:

* equal-height sections
* repetitive layouts
* x-by-y card grids
* standard feature cards
* visual repetition
* predictable landing page patterns

Encourage:

* asymmetry
* sections with varying heights
* large amounts of intentional whitespace
* layouts that breathe
* compositions that feel handcrafted
* different personalities between sections
* unexpected transitions
* layouts that prioritize feeling over efficiency

No two major sections should feel identical.

---

# Storytelling

Think in chapters rather than sections.

The website should feel like an unfolding experience rather than information arranged vertically.

Information should emerge naturally rather than being dumped immediately.

The visitor should feel like they are exploring memories rather than browsing content.

---

# Visual Language

The experience should be cinematic, premium, mystical, and aesthetic.

Potential elements that may or may not be appropriate:

* quote overlays
* poems
* pause moments
* full-screen statements
* handwritten notes
* scrapbook compositions
* mixed media
* overlapping photographs
* torn paper textures
* film strips
* stamps
* postcards
* polaroids
* journal fragments
* captions
* layered typography

These are possibilities rather than requirements.

---

# Memory Fragments

The experience may benefit from feeling fragmented rather than perfectly structured.

Sections can behave like memories, journal pages, or moments.

The narrative does not necessarily need to be linear.

---

# Quotes and Pause Moments

Certain areas may contain little or no information.

These spaces may simply exist to create atmosphere.

Examples include:

* quotes
* poetry
* reflections
* observations
* moments of silence

The purpose of these moments is emotional pacing rather than content density.

---

# Seasonal Themes

Seasonality may be explored if appropriate.

This should be evaluated as part of the overall experience rather than treated as a requirement.

---

# Images

Images will be generated using ChatGPT.

This project does not require image creation.

Instead, image requirements should be documented thoroughly.

For every image, detailed prompts tailored specifically for ChatGPT image generation should be produced.

These prompts should include:

* composition
* camera angle
* lens
* lighting
* mood
* weather
* season
* textures
* foreground and background elements
* color grading
* cinematic references
* most importantly, dimensions in pixels as well as aspect ratio

* typography
